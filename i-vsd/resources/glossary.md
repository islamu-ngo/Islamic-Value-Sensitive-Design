# Glossary

- I-VSD: Islamic Value Sensitive Design, a provider-responsibility design-reasoning framework for software.
- Provider-mediated software solution: software where a provider shapes access, data, defaults, policies, infrastructure, incentives, or governance.
- Software service: product, platform, API, hosted system, self-hostable system, AI tool, or integration with ongoing provider responsibility.
- Value Sensitive Design: a design method for identifying stakeholders, values, and technical implications; in I-VSD it is a sensitivity tool, not the moral source.
- Design heuristic: a provider-facing rule of thumb derived from principles, risks, evidence, and tradeoffs.
- Ethical harm: privacy, security, financial, spiritual, social, reputational, community, accessibility, or operational harm.
- Stakeholder: direct user, indirect affected person, non-user, vulnerable group, administrator, maintainer, partner, future user, or community.
- Dark pattern: interface or product tactic that manipulates users against their interest.
- Enshittification: gradual degradation of product commitments after users or communities become dependent.
- Amanah / Trust: stewardship of data, infrastructure, access, community confidence, support, governance, and admin power.
- Sidq / Truthfulness: accurate claims about pricing, features, privacy, AI, security, open source, limitations, and risks.
- Adl / Justice: fair access, pricing, ranking, moderation, enforcement, support, appeals, and burden distribution.
- La Darar / Non-Harm: duty to avoid unjustified harm and mitigate foreseeable risk.
- Huquq al-Ibad / Rights of People: consent, privacy, property, reputation, dignity, fair dealing, export, deletion, correction, and contestability.
- Riba: interest/usury concerns requiring scholarly review when finance, credit, late fees, payment, investment, or monetization may be implicated.
- Gharar: excessive uncertainty in price, terms, renewal, risk, capability, data use, or dependencies.
- Tadlis / deception concerns: hiding defects, manipulating impressions, manufacturing trust, or presenting false comparisons.
- Haya / Modesty: avoiding unnecessary exposure, immodest incentives, harmful visibility defaults, and attention exploitation.
- Ihsan / Excellence: going beyond minimum compliance in safety, reliability, accessibility, maintainability, docs, and religious trust.
- Tajassus / spying: unjustified collection, inference, tracking, profiling, fingerprinting, monitoring, or surveillance.
- Barakah / blessing: theological concept; never treat it as a measurable KPI or evidence metric.
- Fatwa: qualified Islamic legal opinion; this skill does not issue one.
- Sharia: Islamic law and moral order; do not claim Sharia certification through this skill.
- Sunnah, Hadith, Quran: Islamic source categories; interpretation for rulings belongs to qualified scholarship.
- Maqasid: higher objectives of Islamic law; useful context, not a shortcut around specific evidence.
- Qiyas: analogical reasoning in Islamic jurisprudence; do not perform as a legal ruling.
- Public interest: welfare consideration that requires careful authority boundaries.
- Certainty/contestability: mark whether a recommendation is clear design reasoning, uncertain application, or requires scholarly/expert review.
