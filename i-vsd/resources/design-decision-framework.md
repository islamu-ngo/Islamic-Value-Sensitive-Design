# Design Decision Framework

## Decision Steps

1. State the user job and provider responsibility.
2. Identify likely harms, vulnerable stakeholders, and exploitability.
3. Define protective defaults for data, visibility, payments, content, and risky actions.
4. Check pricing, limits, terms, renewals, dependencies, and risks before commitment.
5. Check consent clarity and separate required from optional choices.
6. Check cultural and religious context without turning preference into a legal ruling.
7. Check accessibility, localization, RTL, and device constraints as Justice and Excellence concerns.
8. Check comparison honesty and claim evidence.
9. Record rejected manipulative alternatives.

## Anti-Patterns

Optimizing satisfaction over welfare, making risky options frictionless and protective choices hidden, burying limits, using spiritual pressure, and treating accessibility as polish.
