# Project Case Patterns

These patterns are illustrative. They show traceability between principles, heuristics, and design decisions. They do not show measured stakeholder experience, production reliability, scholar approval, market viability, or long-term community impact.

## Strategic Patterns

- Explicit moral scope boundary for what the provider will and will not enable.
- User and community safety posture against scams, exploitation, abuse, and false trust signals.
- Privacy-oriented strategy that treats data minimization as a provider responsibility.
- Portability, interoperability, export, or migration direction when users depend on continued access.
- Deployment or tenancy choices that clarify who controls data, policies, and operational duties.
- Context-aware defaults that reduce confusion or foreseeable harm without coercing users.
- Tiered verification or trust signals that distinguish evidence levels without implying certainty.

## Technical Patterns

- Clear authentication and session boundaries that prevent accidental credential exposure.
- Authorization model with inspectable policy decisions and safe fallback behavior.
- API or UI affordances that expose allowed actions from authoritative server-side policy rather than client guesses.
- Tenant, workspace, organization, or account isolation enforced centrally and verified by tests.
- Sensitive-data separation, consent capture, retention limits, and purpose-specific access controls.
- Credential hashing, idempotency, rate limiting, audit logs, and abuse-resistant upload or input handling.
- Observability that supports repair and accountability without logging secrets or excessive personal data.

## UX Patterns

- Reject dark patterns.
- Use neutral free-plan labels.
- Make subscription and cancellation clarity comparable.
- Use evidence-based availability messaging.
- Avoid preselected paid or privacy-invasive options.
- Disclose fees before commitment.
- Show payment breakdowns: seller price, processing fee, platform fee/tax, refund recipient, and timing.
- Keep post-interaction engagement optional and proportionate.
- Treat accessibility/localization as requirements, not polish.

## Business Model Patterns

- Donations, clearly labeled sponsorships, mission-compatible grants, and transparent support.
- Reject selling user data, surveillance ads, hidden fees, artificial scarcity, financing/sponsor dependency, and hidden influence.
- Open-source, self-hosting, export, escrow, or managed-service options when sovereignty and continuity are part of the promise.
- Separate stewardship, hosting, funding, and commercial incentives where possible to reduce lock-in or enshittification risk; treat this as a risk-control hypothesis, not a guarantee.

## How To Use The Case

Use it as an example of implementation traceability and report wording. Do not use it as proof that I-VSD is validated, that users experience the system as ethical, or that the product has religious/legal certification.
