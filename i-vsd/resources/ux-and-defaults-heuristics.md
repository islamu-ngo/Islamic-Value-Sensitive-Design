# UX And Defaults Heuristics

## Heuristics

- Eliminate dark patterns.
- Make pricing, limits, renewals, risks, and dependencies visible before commitment.
- Respect cultural and religious context.
- Make comparative interfaces honest.
- Put user interest over user satisfaction or conversion.
- Use protective defaults for privacy, visibility, payments, and risky actions.

## Checklist

- Is cancellation as clear as subscription?
- Are costs, fees, renewals, dependencies, and limitations visible before commitment?
- Are paid or privacy-invasive options never preselected?
- Are false urgency, false scarcity, guilt, fear, and spiritual pressure absent?
- Are sensitive data and public visibility restrictive by default?
- Are accessibility, localization, RTL, and cultural needs treated as Justice and Excellence requirements?
- Are risky, irreversible, or recurring actions confirmed clearly?

## Evidence Expectations

User flows, screenshots, copy, settings defaults, accessibility results, localization/RTL review, pricing page, cancellation path, and support complaints.

## Anti-Patterns

Hidden fees, confusing consent, hard cancellation, preselected paid options, addictive loops, outrage rewards, spiritual guilt prompts, and public-by-default sensitive content.
