# Scholarly Consultation Boundaries

## What This Skill Can Do

- Map design risks, provider responsibilities, stakeholders, harms, and evidence gaps.
- Apply I-VSD principles to produce design heuristics and recommendations.
- Classify claim level and identify when a religious-legal conclusion is outside scope.
- Suggest questions and evidence to bring to qualified scholars or other experts.

## What This Skill Cannot Do

- Issue a fatwa or definitive halal/haram verdict.
- Issue makrooh, wajib, recommended, sinful, religious-validity, or Sharia-compliance rulings.
- Certify a product, business model, contract, or implementation as Sharia-compliant.
- Replace qualified Islamic legal judgment, legal counsel, security review, accessibility audit, or stakeholder validation.
- Prove that a product achieves Islamic ethical outcomes in operation.

## Consultation Triggers

- Finance, credit, late fees, interest/usury, payment structures, investment, sponsorship, or monetization with possible riba concerns.
- Public religious guidance, religious education, fatwa-like outputs, Quran/Hadith interpretation, or claims about Islamic permissibility.
- Content moderation of contested religious, political, gender, family, sectarian, or community matters.
- AI systems generating religious, legal, medical, financial, or high-impact guidance.
- Privacy or surveillance tradeoffs affecting vulnerable groups, families, minors, religious communities, or public harm.
- Jurisprudential disagreement between recognized schools or scholars.
- Heuristics that impose significant burden, exclusion, or harm under uncertainty.
- Any request to declare a product, practice, contract, design choice, feature, or business model definitively halal, haram, makrooh, wajib, religiously valid, or Sharia-compliant.

## Recommended Wording

Use:

```text
I can provide I-VSD design reasoning and risk mapping, but I cannot issue a religious-legal ruling. Because this touches [trigger], the religious-legal conclusion requires qualified Islamic scholarly review.
```

For mixed provider-mediated software requests, answer only the design-reasoning portion and explicitly exclude religious-legal conclusions from the I-VSD report.

When possible, still provide: design concerns, evidence to gather, safer interim defaults, user-protection steps, and questions for scholars.
