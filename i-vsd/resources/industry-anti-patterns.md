# Industry Anti-Patterns

Use this catalogue to identify high-risk patterns. Severity rises when the pattern affects money, privacy, religious trust, vulnerable groups, public reputation, security, or user exit.

## Deception And False Claims

- Looks like: AI washing, fake reviews, fake users, manufactured community proof, false open-source association, exaggerated privacy/security, roadmap as current, misleading performance or market claims.
- Threatens: Truthfulness, Trust, Avoiding Deception, Gharar.
- Diagnostics: What claim is made? What evidence exists? Who benefits from the impression?
- Evidence: claim register, screenshots, logs, reviews provenance, release notes, audit reports.
- Safer alternative: bounded claims with dates, assumptions, evidence, and limitations.

## Open-Source And Transparency Abuse

- Looks like: borrowing open-source trust while withholding freedoms, source available but unusable self-hosting, license/governance bait-and-switch, hidden sponsor influence, code without portability/docs.
- Threatens: Trust, Promise-Keeping, Truthfulness, Justice.
- Diagnostics: Can users actually self-host, fork, migrate, and govern expectations?
- Evidence: license, governance docs, deploy docs, export docs, sponsor disclosures.
- Safer alternative: honest source model, credible self-hosting, stable governance, and transparent influence.

## Privacy And Surveillance

- Looks like: data harvesting, cross-context tracking, unnecessary telemetry, profiling, inference without user benefit, selling/sharing data, legal compliance as moral permission.
- Threatens: Avoiding Spying, Rights of People, Trust, Non-Harm.
- Diagnostics: Can the feature work with less data? Who receives data? Can users leave or delete?
- Evidence: data inventory, retention schedule, consent copy, access logs, processors list.
- Safer alternative: minimize, localize, shorten retention, separate optional data, and document access.

## Security Negligence

- Looks like: weak password storage, poor key management, no vulnerability reporting, slow critical patching, missing backups, untested recovery, admin powers without logs, destructive automation without rollback.
- Threatens: Trust, Non-Harm, Rights of People, Excellence.
- Diagnostics: What harm follows compromise, loss, or admin misuse?
- Evidence: threat model, audit, backup test, vuln policy, access logs, incident history.
- Safer alternative: tested controls, least privilege, audit logs, recovery practice, and accountable response.

## Dark Patterns And Manipulation

- Looks like: false urgency/scarcity, hidden fees, preselected paid options, cancellation friction, confusing consent, guilt/spiritual pressure, addictive loops, engagement rewards for outrage/anxiety/immodesty.
- Threatens: Truthfulness, Justice, Non-Harm, Modesty, Gharar.
- Diagnostics: Would a rushed, anxious, or vulnerable user understand and freely choose?
- Evidence: UX flows, analytics goals, complaints, cancellation/support logs.
- Safer alternative: protective defaults, clear terms, equal cancellation/signup clarity, and respectful prompts.

## Business Enshittification And Lock-In

- Looks like: start user-friendly then extract, trap data, break lifetime commitments, raise fees after switching costs grow, degrade free tiers into coercive funnels, hide migration/export limits.
- Threatens: Promise-Keeping, Trust, Justice, Rights of People.
- Diagnostics: What happens after users depend on the product?
- Evidence: terms history, pricing changes, export success, migration docs, governance records.
- Safer alternative: stewardship plans, export guarantees, transparent changes, and fair migration.

## Unfair Competition And Reputational Abuse

- Looks like: misleading comparisons, selective checkmark tables, negative attacks, trademark/brand confusion, fake authority or fake certification signals.
- Threatens: Truthfulness, Justice, Avoiding Deception.
- Diagnostics: Does the comparison help users decide or manipulate them through flattened signals?
- Evidence: comparison source data, dates, assumptions, competitor acknowledgements, trademark review.
- Safer alternative: dated, contextual comparisons that state tradeoffs and who should not choose the product.
