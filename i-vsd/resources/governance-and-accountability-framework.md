# Governance And Accountability Framework

## Review Areas

- Policy ownership and change process.
- Decision rights for product, data, moderation, finance, security, and religious-sensitive claims.
- Admin power limits, access approvals, and privileged-access logging.
- Appeals, corrections, and contestability.
- Auditability and public accountability.
- Contribution governance for open-source/community systems.
- Partner, sponsor, investor, and vendor screening.
- Escalation to scholars, stakeholders, security experts, accessibility experts, legal counsel, or operations leads.

## Review Questions

- Who can make harmful decisions, and what constrains them?
- How are conflicts of interest, sponsor pressure, or investor pressure disclosed and controlled?
- Can users appeal or correct decisions that affect reputation, access, money, or community standing?
- Are admin actions justified, limited, and logged?
- Which decisions require outside expertise?

## Evidence Expectations

Governance docs, admin role matrix, access logs, appeal policy, change log, contributor guide, partner screening checklist, escalation policy, and audit records.
