# Business Model Heuristics

## Heuristics

- Avoid riba and ethically compromising funding where applicable.
- Reduce gharar in pricing, renewals, terms, limits, and dependencies.
- Avoid exploitative monetization and surveillance revenue.
- Preserve commitments during business change.
- Evaluate success beyond revenue, growth, and engagement.

## High-Risk Patterns

Selling user data, surveillance ads, artificial scarcity, hidden platform fees, high switching costs, sponsor/investor influence that undermines commitments, lifetime promise reversals, free tier as coercive funnel, and revenue depending on confusion, addiction, surveillance, or exploitation.

## Preferred Patterns

Donations, clearly labeled sponsorships, mission-compatible grants, transparent paid hosting/support, fair-value exchange, and open-source stewardship with honest self-hosting boundaries.

## Review Questions

- Does revenue depend on deception, surveillance, lock-in, confusion, addiction, or spiritual pressure?
- Are fees, renewals, cancellation, refunds, and limits clear before commitment?
- Could investors, sponsors, affiliates, or platform partners pressure the provider to break promises?
- Are riba concerns possible in credit, late fees, investment, payment flows, or financing?
- What will the provider refuse even if competitors grow faster?

## Boundary

Riba, contract permissibility, investment, and sponsorship questions require qualified scholarly review for religious-legal conclusions.
