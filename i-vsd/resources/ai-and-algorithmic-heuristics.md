# AI And Algorithmic Heuristics

## Heuristics

- Disclose AI/automation limits.
- Preserve source integrity.
- Review bias and disparate harm.
- Evaluate source openness by risk context.
- Protect user autonomy in recommendations.
- Require human escalation for high-impact decisions.

## Checklist

- Is AI-generated or AI-assisted content labeled?
- Are hallucination, uncertainty, and failure modes explained?
- Is human escalation available for high-impact decisions?
- Are source, summary, inference, recommendation, and generated output clearly distinguished?
- Are citations required for factual, religious, legal, medical, financial, or decision-critical claims?
- Are feedback loops and biased training data reviewed?
- Are non-personalized, chronological, or user-controlled alternatives available where recommendations affect autonomy?
- Does the design avoid dependency, outrage, spiritual anxiety, compulsive engagement, or destructive automation?

## Evidence Expectations

Model cards or system notes, prompt boundaries, evaluation sets, red-team results, UI labels, audit logs, human review paths, rollback controls, and incident records.

## Boundary

AI religious, legal, medical, financial, or high-impact guidance requires expert escalation. The skill can recommend safeguards; it cannot validate the content as religiously or professionally authoritative.
