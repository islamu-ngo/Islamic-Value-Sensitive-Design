# Data Governance Heuristics

## Heuristics

- Minimize data collection and precision.
- Make data use understandable before collection.
- Separate required and optional data.
- Restrict sharing and preserve portability.
- Review intrusive signals separately.
- Govern retention, deletion, correction, and access.

## Checklist

- What data is collected and why?
- Is each purpose necessary for user-facing functionality or provider responsibility?
- Can the feature work with less data, less precision, shorter retention, local processing, or anonymous mode?
- Is sharing explicit, limited, and recipient-reviewed?
- Can users export, delete, correct, or transfer data?
- Are admin accesses role-restricted, justified, and logged?
- Are device, location, contacts, wallet, filesystem, browser, biometric, environment, or cross-context signals separately justified?

## Evidence Expectations

Data inventory, purpose map, consent copy, retention schedule, access-control model, export/delete flow, privileged-access logs, and incident/audit records for outcome claims.

## Anti-Patterns

Cross-context tracking, profiling for monetization, unnecessary telemetry, hidden sharing, vague “improvement” purposes, and treating legal consent as moral sufficiency.
