# Content Moderation Heuristics

## Heuristics

- Apply standards consistently and explainably.
- Rank harm by severity and urgency.
- Protect community values without arbitrary enforcement.
- Distinguish user controls from provider enforcement.
- Evaluate moderation outcomes over time.

## Checklist

- Are rules clear, findable, and proportionate?
- Are scams, impersonation, credential theft, harassment, exploitation, and security abuse prioritized?
- Are significant moderation decisions documented?
- Are appeals, corrections, and reversals available?
- Are monetization/ranking decisions suspended when credible harm review is pending?
- Are vague rules avoided or clarified with examples?
- Does contested religious, political, family, gender, sectarian, or community moderation require scholarly/stakeholder escalation?

## Evidence Expectations

Policies, enforcement logs, appeal outcomes, moderator guidance, user reports, abuse metrics, ranking/monetization rules, and stakeholder feedback.

## Anti-Patterns

Arbitrary enforcement, hidden moderation, no appeal, engagement incentives that reward outrage, and false neutrality when provider choices shape harm.
