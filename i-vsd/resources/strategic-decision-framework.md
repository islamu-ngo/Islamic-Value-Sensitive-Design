# Strategic Decision Framework

## Review Areas

- Mission and value proposition.
- Curation boundaries and community commitments.
- Funding, investors, sponsors, grants, and revenue pressure.
- Organizational structure and decision rights.
- Open-source posture and stewardship obligations.
- Partnerships and platform dependencies.
- Long-term maintenance, migration, and EOL plans.
- Market positioning and comparison claims.
- Autonomy from unethical pressure.
- Command-oriented success: obey duties rather than assuming ethics guarantees market success.

## Review Questions

- Does strategy depend on morally prohibited or high-risk practices?
- What commitments will become hard to keep under growth, investor, sponsor, or competitive pressure?
- Who can pressure the provider to compromise privacy, truthfulness, moderation, portability, or funding boundaries?
- Does the provider have a shutdown, migration, continuity, or stewardship plan?
- What will the provider reject even if profitable?

## Evidence Expectations

Mission docs, funding agreements, sponsorship policy, partnership terms, governance docs, open-source license/governance, roadmap, public claims, and stewardship plans.
