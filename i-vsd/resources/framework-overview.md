# Framework Overview

I-VSD is an Islamic provider-responsibility framework for software providers who shape data, access, policies, infrastructure, business incentives, user experience, and community effects. It uses Value Sensitive Design as a design-sensitivity method, but the moral source is the selected Sunni Islamic ethical principles, not stakeholder preference alone.

## Scope

Apply I-VSD to web platforms, SaaS, closed-source products, open-source projects, desktop apps, mobile apps, APIs, self-hostable systems, CLI/MCP tools, AI-native systems, plugins, marketplaces, community platforms, internal tools, and infrastructure when the provider continues to influence design, data, governance, availability, policies, incentives, or stakeholder effects.

Do not apply it as a universal legal verdict, fatwa, Sharia certification, product certification, or proof that users experience the product as ethical.

## Value Hierarchy

1. Selected Sunni Islamic sources and derived principles set the normative boundary.
2. Compatible secular standards, laws, accessibility rules, privacy principles, and industry practices may operationalize the boundary when they do not conflict.
3. Legal compliance is not moral sufficiency.
4. User preference, market growth, stakeholder consensus, or convenience cannot override Islamic prohibitions or duties.

## Six Domains

- Strategic: mission, funding, ownership, partnerships, open-source posture, market pressure, long-term stewardship.
- Design: UX, defaults, consent, pricing visibility, accessibility, localization, content controls, persuasive design.
- Technical: architecture, data, security, account/workspace/tenant or local-user isolation, APIs, AI, auth, interoperability, backups, portability, dependencies.
- Operational: support, incidents, moderation, vulnerability handling, migrations, continuity, EOL, promise keeping.
- Governance: decision rights, accountability, appeals, auditability, contribution rules, partner screening, escalation.
- Evaluation: harm reports, complaints, audits, accessibility findings, appeals, support burden, incident metrics, longitudinal review.

## Quick Workflow

1. Scope the solution and deployment model.
2. Identify provider responsibilities and affected stakeholders, including indirect and vulnerable stakeholders.
3. Select relevant principles and review all six domains.
4. Derive provider-facing heuristics from principles, harms, and tradeoffs.
5. Request evidence: docs, UI, code, policies, logs, audits, interviews, metrics.
6. Classify each claim by evidence level.
7. Produce recommendations with severity, rejected alternatives, and validation gaps.
8. Escalate religious-legal, public-harm, security, accessibility, or legal questions when design reasoning is insufficient.

## Output Boundary

Use wording such as: “This recommendation is I-VSD design reasoning traceable to Trust and Non-Harm.” Avoid wording such as: “This product is Islamically compliant,” “This proves harm is prevented,” or “This is Sharia certified.”
