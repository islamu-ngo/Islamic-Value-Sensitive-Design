# Compliance Checks

This is a review aid, not certification. Use “supports,” “concern,” or “requires review,” not “compliant” as a religious or product certification claim.

## Finding Levels

- Pass: evidence supports the heuristic for the reviewed scope.
- Concern: partial evidence, unclear policy, unresolved tradeoff, or implementation gap.
- Fail: design appears to violate a non-negotiable principle or creates serious unresolved harm.
- Not reviewed: outside scope or evidence unavailable.
- Requires scholarly review: design analysis can continue, but a religious-legal conclusion is out of scope.

## Core Categories

- Value source and claim boundaries.
- Data governance and purpose limitation.
- Privacy and avoiding spying.
- Security and resilience.
- AI and algorithmic behavior.
- Marketing and public claims.
- Pricing, cancellation, renewals, and terms.
- Business model, funding, and partner incentives.
- Moderation, appeals, and community protection.
- Accessibility, localization, RTL, and cultural fit.
- Portability, self-hosting, interoperability, and lock-in.
- Operations, support, vulnerability handling, incident response, and EOL.
- Governance, accountability, admin powers, sponsor influence, and escalation.

## Severity Guidance

Treat deception, riba exposure, surveillance monetization, destructive automation, public religious/legal AI claims, security negligence, and vulnerable-group harm as high risk unless evidence narrows the concern.
